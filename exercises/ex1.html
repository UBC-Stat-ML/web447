Setup

  • Imagine a bag with 3 biased coins
  • Coin \(i\in \{0, 1, 2\}\) has bias \(i/2\)—in other words:
    • First coin: bias is \(0/2 = 0\) (i.e. both sides are “tails”)
    • Second coin: bias is \(1/2 = 0.5\) (i.e. standard coin)
    • Third coin: bias is \(2/2 = 1\) (i.e. both sides are “heads”)

  • Consider the following two steps sampling process
    • Step 1: pick one of the three coins, but do not look at it!
    • Step 2: flip the coin 4 times and observe the flips
  • Our “bag of coin” example explores the following probabilistic inference problem:

If you observe 4 heads, what is the probability that you picked the standard coin?
